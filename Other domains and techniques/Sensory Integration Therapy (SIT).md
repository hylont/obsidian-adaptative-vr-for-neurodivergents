>Sensory Integration Therapy is based on [A. Jean Ayres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A._Jean_Ayres "A. Jean Ayres")'s **Sensory Integration Theory**, which proposes that sensory-processing is linked to [emotional regulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emotional_regulation "Emotional regulation"), learning, behavior, and participation in daily life.(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sensory_integration_therapy#cite_note-:2-2) Sensory integration is the process of organizing sensations from the body and environmental stimuli.
