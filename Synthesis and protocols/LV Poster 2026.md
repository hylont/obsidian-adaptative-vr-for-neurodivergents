# Intro / Context

It's the amount people that do not process sensory inputs like what is considered the "norm". According to L. Little & W. Dunn (2016), at least 13% of the entire population have hypo (less) or hyper (more) -sensitivity to what we feel everyday (stimuli). Some call it disorders or impairments, but we call it diversity.
[Insert 8 senses image]
According to experts (Adrian Galiana-Simal et al., 2018), when we process sensory input, our system goes through 4 steps:
- 1 : Registration (the brain receives sensory information from organs) 
- 2 : Modulation (regulation of intensity) 
- 3 : Discrimination (interpretation of its relevance, characteristics and specific qualities) 
- 4 : Response (Response that will lead to a particular behavior and movements)
Neurodiverse, which we can define by "natural variations in brain function that exist across the entire population, encompassing both neurotypical and neurodivergent individuals.” (Vargas-Salas, 2025), are populations known to have Sensory Processing Disorder/Diversity (SPD). The 2 most documented SPD populations, according to litterature and DSM-5 definitions, are the autistic (or ASD for Autistic Spectrum Disorder), which is a neurodevelopmental "disorder" characterized by restricted and repetitive behaviour, deficits in social communication with SPD, and the ADHD which stands for ATTENTION DEFICIT WITH OR WITHOUT HYPERACTIVITY DISORDER (we can call it disorder, as it's know to be severely impairing)

Naturally, as XR developers, there is high chance that SPD individuals will try our experiences: According to more and more experts (Piller 2025, McGowan 2023, Lacôte-Coquereau 2024), the lack of inclusion of sensory particularities in the design process could become an obstacle for XR.
But designing with neurodiversity experts, implementing stimuli preferences and testing out with stakeholders is expensive (Correia 2023).
That's where we come to the rescue
