>“The main objectives of this study are to test, in a controlled study, the efficacy of this VR-MIP to induce relaxation and sense of [[Presence]], and to analyze whether the stimulation of the senses of touch and smell improves the efficacy of this VR-MIP in inducing relaxation and sense of [[Presence]].” (Serrano, p. 2)

## Protocol design

>“four experimental groups was used (see Fig. 3): (1) VR (using only visual and hearing stimuli); (2) VRMIP þ Smell (using visual, hearing, and tactile stimuli); (3) VRMIP þ Touch (using visual, hearing, and olfactory stimuli); (4) VRMIP þ Touch & Smell (using visual, hearing, tactile, and olfactory stimuli).” (Serrano, p. 5)

>“The VR environment simulated a two-story house located in a meadow and called the “House of Relaxation” (Fig. 1). In it, the following MIP for inducing relaxation were included: affective pictures from the International Affective Pictures System (Ito, Cacioppo, & Lang, 1998), Velten's self-statements (Velten, 1968), a video-clip with a relaxing scene, relaxing music and narratives, sounds of nature, and autobiographical recalls.” (Serrano, p. 3)

>“As the olfactory stimulus, a lavender scent, distributed with a ceramic diffuser, a small-unscented candle and water, was used. The literature states that this scent tends to relax because it reduces arousal levels” (Serrano, p. 4)
## Measures

>“Self-Assessment Manikin: SAM [(Bradley & Lang, 1994), Spanish validation by Molto et al. (1999) and Vila et al. (2001)]. This measure is a non-verbal pictorial-assessment technique that directly measures the affective valence, arousal, and dominance associated with a person's affective reaction to a stimuli.” (Serrano, p. 3)

>“[[Presence]] Self-Assessment Manikin: [[Presence]]-SAM (Schneider, Lang, Shin, & Bradley, 2004). This is a 9-point pictorial scale adapted from the SAM measure (Bradley & Lang, 1994). The authors designed the scale to be moderately correlated (r 1⁄4 0.50) with a verbal sense of [[Presence]] scale.” (Serrano, p. 3)
## Results & discussion

>“mood-induction, the relaxation level increased, and the arousal level decreased significantly; likewise, results show significant changes in other emotions (joy, affective valence, anxiety, and sadness).” (Serrano, p. 6)

>“where positive moods increased while negative moods decreased. The results suggest that it is possible to induce different emotions in a controlled way” (Serrano, p. 6)

>“Therefore, the stimulation of touch and/or smell does not appear to significantly improve the state of relaxation. Only a trend was observed: the groups whose sense of touch was stimulated seemed more relaxed.” (Serrano, p. 6)

>“The data do not support the last hypothesis about differences in the sense of [[Presence]] related to the stimulation of the senses of touch and smell. No statistical differences were found between groups” (Serrano, p. 6)

>“to stimulate the senses of touch and smell, artificial grass and lavender scent were used.” (Serrano, p. 7)

## Conclusion

>“The results of this study confirm that this VR-MIP (“House of Relaxation”) is effective in inducing relaxation, and they show that it is possible to induce this emotion in a controlled way” (Serrano, p. 7)

>“Emotions and sense of [[Presence]] do not significantly improve when smell and/or touch were stimulated.” (Serrano, p. 7)

>“Clearly, further research is needed.” (Serrano, p. 7)

# Synthsis

In this study, the authors try to prove that multimodal (visual, haptic, [[Olfactory]]) stimuli increase [[Presence]] and relaxation.
However the stimulation of touch and/or smell does not appear to significantly improve the state of relaxation. It also appears that multiple stimuli had limited negative effects on relaxation, anxiety and sadness. However joy and arousal were a little bit increased.
