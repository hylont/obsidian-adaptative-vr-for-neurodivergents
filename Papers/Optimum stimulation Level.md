# Construct

>People tend to prefer intermediate levels of stimulation, referred as the [[Optimum Stimulation Level (OSL)]]

>There are reliable individual differences in the amount of stimulation considered optimal by a given person, which appear to be affected by social learning factors as well as biochemical substances

>People with higher OSLs engage in exploratory behaviors to a greater extent than people with lower OSLs

# Synthesis

In this short article about consumer behavior, Steenkamp, a pionner in stimulation theory, explains the origins, objectives and methods of measuring Optimal Stimulation Level, or OSL.
The term "optimal" refers to a subjective intermediate level of stimulation, and is affected by learning factors and biochemical substances (?)
5 methods of OSL self-report are listed, along the authors' short form, the [[CSI short-form scale]].
